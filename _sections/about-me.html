I am an MSc Cognitive Science Student at the Ruhr-Universität Bochum, and am a student assistant with Neural and Behavioural data at the Institut für Neuroinformatik, Dynamics of Learning group. I hold a Bachelor of Arts degree in Psychology with a minor in Software Engineering from American University of Central Asia and Bard College. Currently, I am writing my master's thesis on systematic assessment of the effects of ICA-based artefact correction on multivariate pattern analysis of EEG data, under the supervision of Dr Laura-Isabelle Klatt at IfADo.
My research interests are related to computational and cognitive neuroscience of learning, working and episodic memory paradigms. I experienced with EEG, intracranial EEG/LFP data analysis and machine learning.
I am attracted to interdisciplinary studies (i.e. combination of psychology and software engineering, or neuroscience and ML), and I believe that the best kind of science can only be made at the intersection of 2 or more fields.
